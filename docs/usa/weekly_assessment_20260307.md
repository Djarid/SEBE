## THE REFLEXIVITY THESIS: CRITICAL UPDATE

**The single most important data point this week: Treasury yields ROSE
during a major geopolitical crisis.**

The 10-year yield went from ~3.95% pre-strike to 4.098% by 5 March.
Yields briefly fell below 4% on 27 February (stagflation fears, pre-war)
then reversed sharply upward once strikes began.

CNBC headline, 2 March: "Bonds defy safe-haven playbook."

Bloomberg headline, 3 March: "Global Bonds on Knife-Edge as Iran War
Revives Inflation Threat."

Marketplace, 2 March: analysts noting that "normally during geopolitical
conflicts, bond prices rise and yields fall... the opposite has occurred."

**This is the reflexivity coupling in action.** The original wargaming
(v3) proposed that de-dollarisation has fundamentally changed the
flight-to-safety dynamic — that in a post-2022 world where the US has
demonstrated willingness to weaponise the dollar (Russian reserve freeze),
Treasuries no longer function as the automatic safe-haven during crises.
The adversarial review downgraded this from Almost Certain to Highly
Likely because it was "unproven and contradicted by 2008/2022 precedent."

**It is no longer unproven.** We now have a live data point: a major
shooting war involving the United States, and bond investors are selling
Treasuries rather than buying them. The mechanism is exactly as modelled:
oil shock → inflation expectations rise → investors demand higher yields
to compensate → bonds sell off → yields rise → debt service costs increase.

The 2008 and 2022 comparisons are no longer valid rebuttals:
- 2008 was a financial crisis, not a commodity/energy shock
- 2022 was post-COVID with universal weakness; there was no alternative
  to the dollar because everyone was weakened simultaneously
- 2026 is different: the world is bifurcating, alternatives (gold, euro,
  RMB trade settlement) are maturing, and the US is the aggressor in an
  energy-disrupting war

**Revised assessment: Reflexivity coupling probability upgraded from
Highly Likely (65-90%) back to Almost Certain (>90%).** The live market
data now supports the v3 thesis that v2 adversarial review downgraded.

## THE EUROPEAN ENERGY DIMENSION: NEW FINDING

This was flagged as the "energy blind spot" in the original wargaming.
The Iran crisis has exposed it as a primary transmission mechanism, not
a secondary effect.

### European gas crisis

- TTF gas prices nearly doubled in one week (€30 → €60+, settling ~€48)
- Qatar LNG production halted (Iranian drone strikes on facilities)
- European gas storage critically low: 46 bcm (Feb 2026) vs 60 bcm (2025)
  vs 77 bcm (2024) — a 40% deficit vs two years ago
- Europe entering storage refill season with lowest reserves in three years
- Bruegel (EU think tank): "Storage refill operations could be disrupted,
  placing pressure on industrial energy costs"
- European Commission convened emergency technical experts (5 March)
- NPR/Clearview Energy: "about as wrong as things could go at any single
  point of failure" for global oil markets

### The "Reverse Suez" mechanism

This is a new analytical framework developed this week. The 1956 Suez
Crisis saw the US use financial leverage (threatening to sell sterling)
to force the UK to withdraw from Egypt. The 2026 inversion:

**The mechanism is not a coordinated political attack on Treasuries.**
It is a balance-of-payments necessity. European governments facing another
round of €hundreds of billions in energy subsidies (2022 precedent: €650Bn
across the EU) may be forced to liquidate dollar-denominated reserve assets
to fund domestic energy intervention. This is fiscal triage, not financial
warfare — but the effect on Treasury markets is identical.

**Critical nuance on UK Treasury holdings:** The UK's $888.5Bn in US
Treasury holdings is substantially custodial — City of London institutions
holding on behalf of global clients (sovereign wealth funds, corporate
treasuries, foreign pension funds). The US Treasury acknowledges this
"custodial bias." This means:
- The UK government does not have a $888Bn sell button
- BUT the eurodollar entities custodied through the City will independently
  rotate out of Treasuries if confidence cracks
- A distributed, uncoordinated selloff through City infrastructure is
  actually MORE dangerous than a sovereign decision because it cannot be
  negotiated with or prevented
- The same City infrastructure clearing energy derivatives, insurance
  claims, and FX hedges is also custodying the Treasury holdings — the
  energy shock and the financial shock route through the same plumbing

**The European political framing matters:** America's war in Iran is, from
a European fiscal perspective, an indirect attack on European energy
security. The US is energy-independent; Europe is not. The US launched
strikes without NATO invocation or meaningful European consultation. Europe
bears the energy cost of a war it didn't start, didn't want, and wasn't
consulted on. This creates political incentive for European governments to
distance themselves from dollar dependence — not as hostile action but as
rational self-protection.

## UK-SPECIFIC ASSESSMENT

### North Sea: The strategic vulnerability exposed

The Iran crisis has made the UK's energy policy incoherence starkly visible:

- UK North Sea production has declined 72% since 1999 peak
- Zero exploration wells drilled in fiscal 2024-25 (first time ever)
- Major operators (Apache, Ineos) exiting
- UK faces 200%+ growth in LNG import requirements by 2030
- Government policy actively accelerating decline beyond geological rate
- Remaining reserves: up to 7.5Bn barrels technically recoverable (OEUK/
  Westwood), but investment has collapsed
- 80% of North Sea oil exported, not used domestically
- Private operators have no obligation to supply UK consumers first

**The strategic case for state intervention in North Sea production has
moved from theoretical to urgent.** Germany and the Netherlands have
already reversed course and agreed to explore for gas in their North Sea
sectors. The UK is the outlier — winding down domestic production while
its LNG supply routes are disrupted by a war it didn't start.

Options range from emergency requisition powers (fastest) to strategic
reserve obligations (Norwegian SDFI model) to full CNI nationalisation
(most comprehensive but slowest and legally complex). The abolition of
BNOC in 1985 and the failure to create a sovereign wealth fund are now
revealed as catastrophic strategic errors.

### Defence posture

- Starmer's refusal to allow US use of UK bases (Diego Garcia, Fairford)
  is the most significant UK sovereignty assertion since Iraq 2003
- RAF Typhoons flying defensive intercept from Qatar — UK is in theatre
  but not attacking
- E3 statement (UK/France/Germany) calling for ceasefire is a de facto
  break with US policy
- JEF relevance increasing as a UK-led framework independent of US command
- Reform/Conservative pressure to enter the war continues; Starmer holding

### Economic exposure

- UK energy prices spiking (gas prices doubled this week)
- BoE rate cut expectations pushed back (July → September at minimum)
- Sterling under pressure from Gulf instability exposure
- Cost of living crisis deepening
- Net energy importer status now a strategic liability, not merely an
  economic inconvenience

## HISTORICAL FRAMEWORK: THE VOLKER FUND THREAD

Research conducted this week traces the causal chain connecting the current
crisis to its intellectual origins. This is relevant to the analytical
framework because it identifies why the Anglo-American system lacks the
institutional resilience to absorb this shock.

**The chain:** William Volker Fund (1946 onward) → funded Hayek at
Chicago, Mises at NYU, Friedman's *Capitalism and Freedom* lectures, the
founding conference of Mont Pelerin Society (1947) → MPS-connected
personnel (Shultz) enter Nixon White House → gold window closure (1971)
driven by Friedmanite prescription for floating rates → 1973 oil shock
hits unanchored monetary system → stagflation misattributed to Keynesian
demand management (actually caused by reserve currency structural flaw +
exogenous commodity shock) → Thatcher/Reagan implement Chicago School
programme (1979-81) → BNOC abolished (1985), privatisation, financialisation
→ forty years of rent extraction hollows out strategic depth → MAGA emerges
from the wreckage → unfunded war in Iran funded by $38.5T in debt.

**The analytical relevance:** The UK's current energy vulnerability, the
US fiscal position, the absence of sovereign wealth funds, the
financialisation of strategic assets, and the inability to mount a
coordinated response to the current crisis are all traceable to specific
policy choices made within this intellectual framework. The Bretton Woods
design flaw (national currency as reserve) was not resolved but deferred;
the deferred consequences are now arriving under conditions that make them
unmanageable.

**Key correction to standard narrative:** The 1970s stagflation was not a
failure of Keynesian economics. It was a failure of the reserve currency
architecture (Triffin Dilemma) compounded by an exogenous energy shock.
Keynes himself proposed the Bancor (supranational reserve currency) at
Bretton Woods in 1944, which would have avoided the structural flaw. The
US overrode this proposal to secure dollar hegemony. Friedman's framework
was then used to misdiagnose the resulting crisis and install a policy
programme that served capital interests while stripping strategic depth
from both the US and UK economies.
