## Core Thesis

The United States is in structural decline. Bretton Woods (1945) embedded
a self-destruct mechanism (Triffin's Dilemma: the reserve currency issuer
must run deficits to supply the world with dollars, which eventually
undermines confidence in the currency). Keynes warned of this and proposed
the bancor. He was overruled. The 1971 gold window closure removed the
constraint that managed the flaw. The 1981 Mont Pelerin Society neoliberal
turn (Reagan/Thatcher) actively exploited it. Every president since has
operated within the resulting framework. The current crisis is the terminal
phase.

No recovery mechanism has been identified above Remote (<5%). The exit
triggers the same crisis it tries to prevent. Four rounds of adversarial
review failed to debunk the thesis; Round 4 strengthened it.

## The Arithmetic

US gross debt: **$38.56 trillion**, growing $6.43 billion per day.

At **5% average yield**, annual interest reaches **~$1.9 trillion** (half
of all federal revenue). At 6%, it exceeds total income tax revenue. The
argument that rising yields attract capital and stabilise the system fails:
the yield that clears the bond market is a temperature that kills the
patient. This is arithmetic, not opinion.

Every crisis response since 1981 has increased debt and inequality while
reducing institutional capacity. The ratchet turns one way. Post-1971, the
ability to print enabled fake adaptations (covering symptoms, not treating
disease). Recovery would require fiscal austerity, military cuts, social
restructuring and intentional dollar devaluation. No political will exists.
Both major parties are neoliberal. 45 years of Mont Pelerin ideological
conditioning (Volcker Fund, Chicago School, Fox News, Federalist Society)
has made the electorate hostile to every component of the cure.

The cure itself is the final problem. Any devaluation large enough to
restore competitiveness and reduce real debt burden triggers the same
triple cascade (fund exit, stagflation, reflexive S&P repricing) as
unmanaged collapse. The size of the devaluation is irrelevant; the
mechanism activates at any magnitude that matters. **The exit IS the
crisis.**

## Five Crisis Pathways

Three pathways are reflexively coupled (Cluster A); two are semi-independent
(Cluster B) but can trigger Cluster A.

**Cluster A (trigger together):**

1. **Trump/Warsh monetary acceleration.** Warsh cuts rates on a political
   timeline (midterms November 2026, 3-6 month sentiment lag). Trump borrows
   more (developer psychology: cheaper debt = more leverage). Issuance floods
   the market. Foreign demand is insufficient. The Fed absorbs. Soft
   monetisation. Dollar weakens. The Fed is completely trapped: every
   intervention tool (capital controls, yield curve control, swap lines,
   coordinated intervention) destroys the reserve currency confidence it
   tries to protect. Capital controls announce non-convertibility. Yield
   curve control admits bonds cannot sell at market rates. Swap lines require
   international cooperation that Trump has systematically destroyed.
   Coordinated intervention delays 12-18 months then resumes from a worse
   position. **Using the tools confirms the thesis. Not using them allows it
   to play out.**

2. **AI bubble collapse.** The S&P 500 is inflated by circular revenue
   flows across the entire Magnificent Seven. Microsoft invests in OpenAI.
   OpenAI buys Azure. Nvidia invests in OpenAI. OpenAI buys Nvidia chips.
   AMD pays OpenAI in equity to become a customer. Google, Amazon, Meta,
   Oracle and Tesla are all making the same bet on the same infrastructure.
   37% of the S&P 500, highest concentration in 145 years. The "diversified
   buyers" are diversified across participants in the same bubble.
   Additionally, the AI product destroys its own market: it eliminates the
   employees whose productivity generates the subscription revenue that funds
   the AI services. The S&P crash and dollar crisis are reflexively coupled
   (foreign equity exit forces simultaneous dollar selling through the same
   transaction).

3. **Fund divestment cascade.** Nordic and Dutch pension funds (Norway GPFG
   ~$1.7T, Dutch ABP ~$600B, Swedish AP funds ~$200B; all JEF-aligned, all
   with established ESG divestment frameworks) are the most likely source.
   The first move is quiet: routine rebalancing that reduces US weighting,
   disclosed in quarterly reporting. The public trigger comes when someone
   names the pattern. Norway's GPFG is the largest but most diplomatically
   constrained (NATO, Arctic); a Swedish or Dutch fund is more likely to
   move first. Fiduciary duty accelerates action when both equity and
   currency legs are moving simultaneously.

**Cluster B (can trigger Cluster A):**

4. **ICE escalation / civil unrest.** ICE is funded outside congressional
   appropriations ($75B via OBBBA, bypassing the foundational check of the
   power of the purse). Military command staff purged for loyalty. Escalation
   produces mass protests, Insurrection Act, constitutional crisis, capital
   flight. Cluster A engages.

5. **Greenland annexation.** Provides governance justification for European
   fund divestment. Fractures NATO. Accelerates European strategic autonomy.

**Pathway 6 (AI employment displacement)** operates on a longer timeline
but reinforces the crisis. AI is automating the final quadrant of human
economic value (non-routine cognitive). Previous transitions moved workers
between quadrants. This one automates the top. The question is capability,
not palatability: if a task can be automated, economic pressure ensures it
will be. Accountability, legitimacy and physical presence are constraints
on deployment, not capability. Deployment constraints erode over time, as
in every previous quadrant. There is no fifth quadrant. **"AI delivers" is
an additional crisis pathway, not a circuit-breaker.**

## Alternative Systems

The dollar's defence ("nowhere else to go") is rusting. Wero is live with
47 million users, expanding to 130 million by 2027. CIPS is operational.
BRICS bilateral settlement is active (oil traded in yuan). The Digital
Euro is in development. These do not need to replace the dollar. They need
to weaken the flight-to-safety reflex during the next crisis. In 2008 and
2022, alternatives did not exist. In 2026, they do.

Separately, European defence cooperation (JEF, 3% GDP targets, domestic
supply chains) is reducing the security justification for holding dollar
reserves. Nations that do not depend on the US for defence have less
reason to hold dollars to maintain Washington's favour.

Each window has lower survival probability because muddle-through worsens
fundamentals. Debt grows +$2.3 trillion per year (~$77 billion additional
annual interest per cycle). Privilege erodes. The S&P concentrates further.

year 2 makes year 3 harder.

Step-down multipliers are anchored to debt accumulation arithmetic.
Uncertainty bands widen at longer horizons to reflect compounding
imprecision and the possibility of unforeseen events (which add variance
in both directions without changing the central estimate).

## UK Position

The question is not "can the UK decouple?" It cannot. The structural
entanglement is too deep.

| Channel | Exposure |
|---------|----------|
| Financial | ~$500B+ UK institutional holdings in US equities. London is world's largest offshore dollar clearing centre. |
| Trade | ~£250B bilateral (US is UK's largest single-country trading partner) |
| Intelligence/Military | Five Eyes, NATO, Trident (warheads US-serviced) |
| Technology | UK public and private sector runs on US cloud (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) |

The correct question is: how deep is the drag? The UK is dragged down by
US decline (**Highly Likely, 65-90%**). Mitigating factors (JEF, domestic
defence supply chain, BoE independence) may reduce severity but cannot
prevent impact. AI employment displacement hits the UK on the same
timeline. The UK has no more institutional capacity to manage 20%+
white-collar unemployment than the US does.

## Falsification

The assessment would be revised toward stability if any of the following
emerged:

- Treasury bid-to-cover ratios increasing
- Dollar reserve share stabilising for 4+ consecutive quarters
- Federal deficit narrowing
- S&P 500 breadth widening (equal-weight outperforming cap-weight)
- Real wage growth exceeding inflation for 4+ consecutive quarters
- Bipartisan fiscal framework agreement
- Warsh demonstrating genuine Fed independence
- Central bank gold buying decelerating

**None are present. All trends are adverse.**
