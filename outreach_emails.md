## 1. Resolution Foundation

**To:** info@resolutionfoundation.org
**Note:** economy@resolutionfoundation.org bounced (550 reject, not a valid recipient). Use info@ only.
**Subject:** Infrastructure-based taxation for post-employment fiscal sustainability

Dear Resolution Foundation research team,

I am writing to share a fiscal policy proposal that addresses a problem
your work on living standards and tax policy has repeatedly identified:
the erosion of employment-linked tax revenue as automation scales.

The Sovereign Energy and Bandwidth Excise (SEBE) is a mechanism for
taxing the physical infrastructure of automated production (energy
consumption and data throughput) rather than the labour it displaces.
It generates an estimated £34-46 billion at launch in 2030 (2026 prices),
growing automatically with automation to £93 billion by 2040. The
attached summary and full academic brief set out the mechanism, rate
structures, revenue model and implementation pathway.

Three aspects may be relevant to the Foundation's current work
programme:

1. **Fiscal sustainability.** SEBE revenue scales with the problem
   it addresses. As automation erodes income tax and NI receipts, SEBE
   grows because the same automation consumes more energy and bandwidth.
   The crossover (SEBE revenue exceeding income tax losses) occurs in the
   early 2040s at projected growth rates.

2. **Tax reform.** SEBE is measured at physical infrastructure (power
   meters, internet exchange points), making it simpler to administer and
   harder to evade than corporate income tax. Administrative cost is
   estimated below 1% of revenue.

3. **Living standards.** The proposal includes an illustrative two-stage
   distribution model (UBI ramping to Universal Living Income), though
   SEBE revenue is general-purpose and could fund any policy priority.

The full working paper (v3.0), revenue model and cost model are
available at https://github.com/jasonhuxley/SEBE. I have also submitted
the proposal to the Green Party's Policy Development Committee and to
IPPR.

I would welcome any engagement, whether a brief exchange, a critique
of the revenue assumptions, or consideration for the Foundation's
research pipeline.

## 2. New Economics Foundation (NEF)

**To:** info@neweconomics.org
**Subject:** SEBE: a revenue mechanism for Living Income provision

Dear NEF team,

I am writing because your Living Income campaign and SEBE solve
different halves of the same problem. Your work has established what a
decent minimum income looks like and why the current social security
system fails to provide it. SEBE provides a mechanism to fund it at
scale, without depending on the employment-linked tax base that
automation is eroding.

The Sovereign Energy and Bandwidth Excise taxes the physical
infrastructure of automated production: energy consumption (kWh) at
commercial facilities and cross-border data throughput (TB) at internet
exchange points. Revenue estimate: £34-46 billion at launch in 2030
(2026 prices), growing to £93 billion by 2040 and £159 billion by 2045
as automation scales. The tax is self-scaling: more automation means
more energy consumption means more revenue, precisely when it is
needed most.

The attached summary sets out the mechanism. The full academic brief
includes an illustrative two-stage distribution model that ramps from
a modest UBI (starting ~£650/adult/year at launch, growing with
revenue) toward a Universal Living Income of £29,000/adult/year, plus
Universal Basic Services worth £2,500/person/year. The combined living
standard (£31,500/person/year) is derived from median full-time
take-home pay.

This is not a speculative thought piece. The revenue model is built
from DESNZ energy consumption data, Ofcom throughput figures and data
centre economics. Rate structures, tiering, evasion countermeasures
and implementation timelines are specified. The full working paper
(v3.0) and all supporting models are at
https://github.com/jasonhuxley/SEBE.

I have submitted the proposal to the Green Party's Policy Development
Committee and to IPPR. I would welcome any engagement from NEF,
particularly given the natural alignment between SEBE's revenue
mechanism and your Living Income framework.

Yours sincerely,
